# CONQUEST: The Official Comprehensive Rulebook

Welcome to **CONQUEST** (formerly known as Battle Arena). This rulebook is designed to be completely exhaustive. Even if you have never played a board game in your life, reading this guide from start to finish will teach you exactly how to play, how to fight, and how to win.

---

## 1. The Goal of the Game
Conquest is a race of survival and combat. You and your opponents begin at **Tile 1 (The Frontier)**. You will roll dice to travel across a 125-tile board. Along the way, you will gather magical items, fall into dangerous traps, and fight terrifying monsters.

The ultimate goal is to reach **Tile 125: The Battle Arena**. Once inside the Arena, the race ends, and a final, brutal fight between the players begins. The last player left alive in the Arena wins the game.

---

## 2. Components and Setup
Before you begin, you need to set up the physical game space.

### What You Need
* **The Board:** A 125-tile pathway divided into 4 Zones (Frontier, Overgrowth, Cursed Ruins, Citadel).
* **Player Pawns:** One unique token or figure for each player to track their position.
* **A Six-Sided Die (1d6):** A standard die numbered 1 through 6.
* **Tracking Paper/App:** Something to track every player's Health Points (HP).
* **Relic Deck:** Weapons, potions, armor, spells.
* **Fate Deck:** Board-wide random events.
* **Standard Monster Deck:** Basic enemies.
* **Boss Monster Deck:** Major, multi-round enemies.

### How to Set Up
1.  **Shuffle the Decks:** Thoroughly mix each of the four card decks individually. Place them face-down next to the board. Leave space next to each deck for a Discard Pile.
2.  **Place Players:** Every player places their pawn on Tile 1.
3.  **Starting Stats:** Every player begins with 100 HP and 0 cards.
4.  **The Merchant’s Shop:** Take the top 3 cards from the Relic Deck and place them face-up near the board. This is the Shop.
5.  **Determine Turn Order:** Everyone rolls the die. The highest number goes first. Play proceeds clockwise (to the left).

---

## 3. The Golden Rules of Play
These three rules supersede everything else in the game.

> **The Health Limit:** Your HP can never go above 100. If you have 90 HP and drink a +20 HP potion, you simply stop at 100.
> 
> **The Hand Limit:** You may never hold more than 5 Relic Cards in your hand. If you ever draw a 6th card, you must immediately look at your hand, choose one card, and put it in the Relic Discard Pile.
> 
> **Death:** If your HP ever reaches 0, your character dies. You are eliminated from the game. Any cards in your hand are placed in the Relic Discard pile.

---

## 4. How to Take a Turn (Movement)
When it is your turn, you must follow these steps in exact order:

### Step A: Roll to Move
Roll the six-sided die. Move your pawn forward that exact number of spaces. You must move forward unless a specific card or trap forces you backward.

* **The "Sprint" Rule:** Exactly once per game, you may declare a Sprint before you roll. You roll two six-sided dice instead of one and add them together. Warning: If you Sprint, you forfeit the right to draw any cards or use a Merchant on that turn, even if you land on them! Keep track of who has used their Sprint.

### Step B: Item Sacrifice (Altering Fate)
This is a game of hand management. At the exact moment you roll your die, you can choose to "Sacrifice" (throw away into the discard pile) cards from your hand to bend the rules:

* **Adjust Movement:** Discard 1 card from your hand to add +1 or subtract -1 from your die roll. You can do this to ensure you land on a good tile or miss a bad one.
* **Evade a Trap:** If your final movement lands you on a Trap tile, you can immediately discard 2 cards from your hand to "Evade." You take zero damage and ignore the trap entirely.

### Step C: Resolve the Tile
Look at the tile you landed on. You must immediately do whatever that tile dictates. Once the tile's effect is complete, your turn is over, and the player to your left goes.

## 5. Board Spaces and Interactions
Every tile on the board has a specific function. When your pawn stops moving, check this list:

| Tile Type | Effect |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Safe Tiles (Blank)** | Nothing happens. You rest safely until your next turn. |
| **Treasure Cache (Mystery Box)** | Draw 1 card from the top of the Relic Deck and add it to your hand. |
| **Oracle/Fate Tile (!)** | Draw 1 card from the Fate Deck. Read it out loud. Discard it after reading. |
| **Trap Tiles** | Take the damage or penalty listed (unless you discard 2 cards to Evade). |
| **Standard Monster (Skull)** | Draw a Standard Monster card. Roll the die to meet or beat the Target Roll to get the reward. Fail and take the penalty. |
| **Boss Monster (Gatekeeper)** | Draw a Boss card. This triggers a multi-round Combat Clash (See Section 6). |
| **The Alchemist/Merchant** | Discard any 2 cards from your hand to take 1 of the 3 face-up Shop cards. Immediately draw a replacement from the Relic Deck. |
| **Campfire** | Choose to either restore 10 HP **OR** draw 1 Relic Card. |
| **Shrine** | Roll the die. On 1-3, lose 10 HP. On 4-6, your next Standard Monster is automatically defeated. |
| **Ambush!** | Draw a Standard Monster. You automatically take its penalty before rolling to see if you can kill it for the reward. |
| **The Toll Bridge** | Discard 1 Relic card as a toll. If you have no cards or refuse to pay, move 5 tiles backward. |

## 6. The Combat System (The Clash)
When you land on a Boss Monster—or when two players fight in the Arena—the normal game pauses. You enter Combat.

Combat happens in Rounds. Every round, combatants have **3 Action Points (AP)**. 

### Your Combat Options (AP Costs)
You can mix and match these options as long as you do not spend more than 3 AP total in one round.

* **Punch/Basic Attack (1 AP):** Deal 10 Damage. You can do this multiple times.
* **Desperate Throw (1 AP):** Discard any 1 Relic card from your hand into the discard pile to deal an instant 15 Damage.
* **Defend (1 AP):** You brace yourself. All incoming damage dealt to you this round is reduced by half (50%, rounded down).
* **Use a Weapon Card (2 or 3 AP):** Play a weapon from your hand. Read the card for AP cost and damage. Weapon cards are discarded immediately after use.
* **Heal (2 AP):** Play a potion/healing card from your hand to gain HP.

### How a Boss Fight Works (PvE)
1.  **Read the Boss Card:** Note its HP, special rules, and its "Action" (what it will do to you).
2.  **Declare Actions:** Decide how you will spend your 3 AP. 
3.  **Simultaneous Resolution:** Damage happens at the exact same time. Subtract the damage you dealt from the Boss's HP, and subtract the Boss's incoming damage from your HP (cutting it in half if you Defended).
4.  **Next Round:** If both you and the Boss are still alive, a new round begins. You get a fresh 3 AP to spend.

### Fleeing a Boss Fight
If you realize you are going to die, you can declare you are Fleeing at the start of any round. Combat ends immediately. You survive, but you must take the "Penalty" listed on the Boss card.

## 7. Player vs. Player (PvP) and The Battle Arena
Players cannot attack each other on the same tile during the race unless they use a specific Ranged Weapon card. However, once a player reaches Tile 125, the endgame begins.

### Entering the Arena
* You do not need an exact roll to land on Tile 125. Any roll that meets or exceeds the required distance stops you at the Arena.
* If you are the first player to arrive, you become the Arena Champion. You must wait for the other players to arrive and cannot move anymore.

### The PvP Clash
When two or more players are in the Arena, they fight using the exact same 3 AP system used for Bosses, but with a twist: **Hidden Actions**.

1.  **The Secret Choice:** Both players secretly decide how they will spend their 3 AP. They place the physical cards they plan to use face-down. *(Use a token, paper, or fingers to signify Punch/Defend).*
2.  **The Reveal:** Count down from 3. On "Go!", both players reveal their actions simultaneously.
3.  **The Math:** Calculate and apply the damage simultaneously. 

### Arena Specific Rules
* **Arena Healing Tax:** Healing cards cost **3 AP** inside the Arena (instead of 2 AP). If you want to heal, you must spend your entire turn doing it; you cannot attack and heal in the same round.
* **Reactions:** Some cards (like Shields or Invisibility Dust) specify they are "Reactions." These are the only cards you can play out of turn to counter a revealed attack.

### Mutual Destruction (Sudden Death)
Because damage is simultaneous, two players can drop to 0 HP at the exact same time. If this happens, it is a Mutual Destruction Tie-Breaker:

1.  Both players immediately discard their entire hands.
2.  Each player rolls the six-sided die.
3.  The player with the highest roll miraculously survives with 1 HP. The lower roller dies. (Reroll any ties).

**The last player standing in the Arena is declared the ultimate victor of Conquest!**
